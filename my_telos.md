- The Process
  - Intake
    - speed at which I can conjure clever imagery to associate information with memory
    - fidelity: how acurately I can soak in fine details of information to reproduce later
  - Encoding
    - speed
    - quality, strength of associations to stick information to memory
    - quantity
  - Recall
    - speed
    - accuracy
  - Retention
    - speed
    - accuracy
- Measuring - Efficiency of Recall
  Focus is directional — it's about what you're pointing your attention at. Choosing a target. It's the initial act of orienting toward something.
  Concentration is durational — it's about sustaining that attention over time. Holding it there against resistance ---distraction, fatigue, boredom---.
  So focus is more like aiming, concentration is more like holding steady.
  In practice: you can focus quickly but struggle to concentrate ---easily drawn in, hard to stay---. Or you can concentrate deeply once locked in but have trouble focusing ---hard to start or switch targets---.
